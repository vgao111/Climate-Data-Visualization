About This Visualization

Motivation

We wanted to make the abstract reality of climate change personally tangible. Global averages hide enormous regional variation — some places have warmed three times faster than others. By letting users click their own country or any country they're curious about, we turn a planetary dataset into a local story.

Design Rationale

A choropleth world map was chosen as the entry point because spatial context is the most natural frame for global data. Countries are colored by their 2000–2014 mean warming anomaly relative to the 1850–1900 pre-industrial baseline, using a yellow→red sequential scale that intuitively encodes increasing heat. Clicking a country reveals either a full annual anomaly time series (with a 10-year rolling mean to smooth noise) or a decade-by-decade bar chart. A year range slider allows users to focus on any sub-period — e.g. just the post-industrial era — enabling comparisons a static view cannot support. Data are pre-aggregated from CMIP6 CESM2 historical simulations using area-weighted country masks via regionmask.
